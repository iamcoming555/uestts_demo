UESTTS:Unifying Emotion Understanding and Speech Generation in Zero-Shot TTS via LLM-Derived Emotion Representations

Abstract: Large language model-based zero-shot text-to-speech has advanced speaker cloning and prosody modeling, yet it often fails to consistently align emotion with textual semantics, especially when the reference speech conflicts with the target-text context, leading to emotional drift, intensity distortion, and cross-utterance inconsistency. We propose UESTTS, a zero-shot emotional TTS framework that jointly performs text-level affect understanding and autoregressive speech-token generation with a text LLM backbone. An Emotion Encoder derives an emotion distribution and a global emotion vector from the LLM hidden states; these affective cues, together with a de-emotionalized style vector, are injected into token generation, while FiLM modulation conditioned on the global emotion vector further improves emotional consistency. UESTTS also introduces hierarchical emotion conditioning, combining paragraph-/long-range trajectory modeling with fine-grained local intensity control. Experiments show that UESTTS preserves zero-shot speaker timbre reproduction while producing emotion that more reliably matches the communicative intent of the input text.
